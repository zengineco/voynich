═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
THE RULE
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

The pattern, stated as a grammar:

  e-grade   → takes -y suffix ~80% of the time
  ee-grade  → takes -y suffix ~75-90% of the time
  eo-grade  → takes -l suffix (~25%) and -dy suffix (~15%); never -y
  o-grade   → takes bare (15-25%) and -aiin (10-25%); rarely -y
  a-grade   → takes bare and -r

The grade and suffix together form a unit. They are not freely
combinable. The Voynich morphology has FUSED axes.

  e + y     = the canonical process predicate (chedy, qokedy, shedy)
  o + bare  = the canonical substance noun (chor, chol, chod)
  o + aiin  = a substance-with-suffix form (chodaiin, qokchodaiin)
  eo + l    = a specific eo-graded structure (cheol, sheol)
  ee + y    = intensive process predicate (cheedy, qokeedy)

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
WHAT THIS MEANS FOR THE 3-AXIS MODEL
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

The 3-axis model was: prefix × grade × suffix as three independent dimensions.
The reality: prefix × {grade-suffix unit} as TWO independent dimensions.

The grade and suffix are bound together into a single inflectional unit.
This is exactly what Semitic morphology does — the binyan in Hebrew is
a pattern that fuses vowel scheme and affix into a single inflectional
template. Hebrew piel, hifil, niphal, etc. each combine vowel pattern
AND prefix/suffix as one inflectional choice.

The Voynich morphology is BINYAN-LIKE. Each token is:
  PREFIX + [VOWEL-PATTERN-AND-SUFFIX-AS-ONE-UNIT] + ROOT-CONSONANTS

This is a major refinement of our understanding. The morphological
system isn't 3 free axes. It's prefix + binyan + root.
