H3d RESULTS — Semantic ground-truth test, f30v borage

The test we ran: if chor encodes "active quality" (hot/cold dimension)
and chol encodes "passive quality" (dry/moist dimension), then Voynich
f30v (identified as Borago officinalis in ZL3b metadata) should match
Avicenna's Canon Book II entry for borage:

  Canon Book II, entry 12 (Lisan al-thaur, Borage):
  "moderate in temperament slightly inclining towards hotness. Its
   moistness is in the last phase of the first degree."

Three predictions, three results:

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
PREDICTION 1 — Both chor and chol present.    PASS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
  f30v contains 6 chor-bearing tokens and 1 chol-bearing token.
  Both dimensions are stated, as Avicenna's prose requires.

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
PREDICTION 2 — Mild/moderate density.   FAIL
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
  f30v chor density: 95.2 per thousand tokens — top 5 of 91 herbal-A folios
  f30v chor density / herbal-A median: 2.4x
  
  Avicenna says borage is "moderate." The Voynich shows borage at
  near-MAX chor density. This contradicts a simple "density = degree"
  reading.

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
PREDICTION 3 — Wrapper morphology matches mild plants.   FAIL
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
  f30v chor-wrappers overlap with MILD folios at 50%
  f30v chor-wrappers overlap with STRONG folios at 100%
  
  f30v patterns morphologically with STRONG-active-quality plants,
  not mild ones.

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
HONEST INTERPRETATION
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

The simple mapping {chor = active quality, density = degree} does
NOT survive contact with f30v.

The H3 result remains intact and unaffected:
  • chor and chol share an inflectional system (r ≈ 1.0)
  • They are paired roots in a productive paradigm
  • 34 minimal pairs confirm this

What H3d shows is that the paradigm does NOT straightforwardly encode
Avicennan quality-and-degree as we predicted. Possible explanations:

(a) chor and chol encode something else (not quality + degree but
    some other paired-dimension medical concept). The pairing is
    real but the SEMANTIC content is something other than what we
    guessed.

(b) The Voynich author followed a different botanical tradition than
    Avicenna's. Khuzi, cited in Canon Book II, rates borage as
    "cold and moist in second degree" — strongly cold. If Voynich
    follows Khuzi-school or some other authority, predictions based
    on Avicenna would fail systematically.

(c) f30v's borage identification, though listed in ZL3b metadata,
    may not be reliable enough to anchor a semantic test. The
    botanical literature has multiple proposed identifications for
    most Voynich folios.

(d) The density signal is not 'degree of quality' but 'rate of
    quality-mention,' which depends on how many therapeutic
    properties Avicenna attributed to the plant — borage was used
    for many things (melancholy, palpitation, jaundice, fevers),
    so it gets many quality-mentions, not strong quality.

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
WHAT WE CAN STATE NOW
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

LOCKED (from H3): chor and chol form a parallel-paradigm inflectional
system with r ≈ 1.0 morpheme-frequency correlation. This is the
strongest distributional finding in the project so far.

REFUTED: The naive Avicennan {chor = active, chol = passive, density
= degree} mapping. f30v borage does not match Avicenna's "moderate"
rating.

NOT YET TESTED: Other plant identifications. We have one anchor;
we need 5-10 to draw any conclusion about whether the encoding
parallels Canon at all. Single-folio results cannot distinguish
"the system doesn't encode Canon" from "this one identification
disagrees with Canon."

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
WHAT TO DO NEXT
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

The disconfirming result does NOT undo the H3 paradigm finding. We
have a real grammar; we just don't know what its substantive content
encodes.

To make progress on semantics specifically, we need:
  1. More plant identifications. Touwaide, Sherwood, Velinska,
     and Tucker have proposed IDs for many folios.
  2. Triangulation: 5+ plant-ID anchors with Canon temperaments,
     measure rank correlation between Voynich morphology and
     Canon temperaments across the set.
  3. Comparison to non-Canon herbal traditions (Dioscorides, the
     Tractatus de Herbis tradition).

But the paradigm-discovery finding doesn't require any of that. We
can publish "Voynich substance lexicon shows a parallel-paradigm
inflectional system with chor and chol as paired roots" without
making any semantic claim.
