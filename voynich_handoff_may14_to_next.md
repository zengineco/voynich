## WHERE TO PICK UP — THREE PRIORITY OPTIONS VINCE SELECTED

### **Option 1 — Sharpen and lock cthor / cthol**

Round 2 left these two equivocal (cthor 70%, cthol 50%). During Round 2 scoring, Vince raised but did not commit a **2×2 grid refinement**:
  - cthor = sharp / directed-outward (not just angular)
  - cthol = broad / flat (foliar)
  - The two are not opposites; they are orthogonal axes that can co-occur

The cleanest test: pull mixed-cth folios from herbal-A where both cthor AND cthol are present in non-trivial counts. Pre-register the prediction that those folios show BOTH features (sharp-edged AND broad-laminar — e.g., dentate margins on a flat lamina, or pointed tips on a broad leaf). Pre-register threshold ≥70% for STRONG. Vince scores blind from a randomized list. Run.

This is a hypothesis-rescue test for cthol and a refinement test for cthor. Honest failure mode: if mixed folios don't show both features, the 2×2 grid is dead and cthol stays at boundary.

### **Option 2 — Test cthody / ctheody separately from cthey**

Round 1 and Round 2 lumped cthody, ctheody, and cthey into a single "rounded" category. Vince's Stage 2A observation introduced the **scale-invariance** refinement: the repeating-parabola gestalt appears at whatever scale the plant calls for (leaf margin, leaflet shape, or whole-leaf stacking). This was never formally tested.

The clean test: separate the three forms and pre-register **distinct** predictions:
  - cthey → small rounded leaflets, leaflet-scale
  - cthody → rounded units at intermediate scale (whole-leaf shape or fruit clusters)
  - ctheody → rounded units at coarse/whole-plant scale

If these three forms cleanly partition by scale, that's a much stronger anchoring (sub-classification within a category). If they don't, the category is "rounded gestalt" undifferentiated and the simpler reading wins.

Folios already named in evidence: f13v, f20r, f21r, f51v, f53v, f54r (cthody-family from Stage 2A). New blind sample needed for replication.

### **Option 4 — Extend gestalt anchoring to lk- (stars) and cheo/sheo (pharm)**

The cth- paradigm has a gestalt mapping for herbal-A subject matter (leaves). The other locked paradigms cover other subject matter. Test whether each paradigm encodes gestalt-like categories of its OWN subject matter:

- **lk- in stars section** (5 forms: lkeey, lkain, lkaiin, lkeedy, lkar) — do these partition star icons by visual gestalt categories? ZL3b metadata available: color (dark/light/medium), point count (6/7/8), dotted, tail. Today's test showed lk- × tail is non-specific (other paradigms also hit). But a gestalt-mapping test with PROPER pre-registration of what each lk- form predicts about icon morphology has never been run. Star-icon properties are richer than "tail vs no-tail" — they include placement, ring (inner/outer), and adjacency. There are 326 star-paragraphs / 10,796 tokens to work with.
- **cheo/sheo in pharm** (12 forms) — do these partition by recipe-action type? Position within recipe (initial/medial/final), adjacency to numeric quantities, adjacency to plant labels (Lf). The Battery D finding locked eo-paradigm as recipe-only morphology. The next question: WITHIN recipes, do cheol/sheol/cheor/etc. distinguish prescriptive sub-actions?

These tests will be harder to score blindly than cth- (since you can't just look at a star icon and say "rounded leaflets" — the categories themselves are unknown). The protocol design itself is the first work.
