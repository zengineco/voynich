A NEW INFERENCE — three roots, not two
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Earlier we noticed chody also exists. If chor/chol/chody all share
the same wrapping inventory, we have a 3-root paradigm.

Quick check on chody: it's much rarer than chor/chol. But chody is
itself a paradigm member — it's the chedy-root in o-grade. And we
locked the o/e contrariety at 40 of 40 pairs in the previous session.

So the wider picture:

  o-grade paradigm (substance-section register):
    chor   (root 1)
    chol   (root 2)
    chody  (root 3, paradigm-rare)
    [...]

  e-grade paradigm (process-section register):
    cher   (would be the e-grade of chor — doesn't appear)
    chel   (would be the e-grade of chol — doesn't appear)
    chedy  (e-grade of chody, the dominant process-register form)

This is interesting. chor and chol are HIGHLY frequent in o-grade
(substance register) but their predicted e-grade equivalents (cher,
chel) do NOT appear. chody is rare in o-grade but its e-grade
equivalent chedy is dominant.

That asymmetry is itself diagnostic. The roots aren't symmetric
across grades — only chedy/chody is paradigmatic. chor and chol
are o-grade only.

Tentative reading: chor and chol are SUBSTANCE-CLASS quality roots
that exist only in the substance register because they describe
attributes of substances. chedy/chody describes a PROCESS that has
both o-grade (substance-affected) and e-grade (process-active) forms.
