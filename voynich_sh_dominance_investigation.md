## §REVISED FINDING (LOCKED)

GPT's intuition that the SH-dom folios "share something" is CORRECT, but the mechanism is different from "stable-state ontology":

**CH-dominant folios are morphologically ELABORATE:**
- Heavy prefix decoration (cth-, qok-, qot-, ot-, d- on substance bases)
- Frequent humor-quality grade words (chor, chol, cthor, dchor)
- Many connectors (daiin, ol, or)
- Likely full substance entries with extensive grade-marking

**SH-dominant folios are morphologically SPARSE:**
- Bare or short-suffix forms (qoty, shdy, chdy, shky, shee)
- Less prefix decoration (28.5% vs 35.8%)
- Less daiin connector
- Likely brief/minimal entries

**Possible interpretations:**
- **A: Entry-length difference**: CH-strong folios contain MAJOR detailed substance entries (de simplicibus chapter heads); SH-dom contain MINOR or SUMMARY entries
- **B: Entry-type difference**: CH-strong entries describe substances at quality-grade level; SH-dom entries name substances without grade analysis
- **C: Section-internal subdomain**: herbal-A may have two sub-genres (full-entry vs label-entry style) that map onto the CH:SH split

NOT confirmed:
- SH ≠ "stable-state ontology" at the morphological/paradigm level
- SH ≠ "less process vocabulary"
- SH ≠ different aspect system

### What this means for the architecture

The 2-humor model (CH and SH as parallel humor-quality bases) **stands intact**. Both bases use the same paradigm. The folio-level CH:SH balance reflects WHICH HUMOR a substance manifests, not WHAT KIND of language is used to describe it.

The morphological-elaboration difference between CH-strong and SH-dom folios is a **separate signal** — probably about ENTRY GENRE, not about the humor itself.

## §IMPORTANT METHODOLOGICAL POINT

The original T16 inverted finding ("folios specialize in humor-pair vocabulary") was correct.

GPT's structural intuition that SH-dom folios "share something" was correct.

The semantic translation (SH = stable-state vs CH = transformable) is NOT supported by the morphological data. It's a hypothesis that the corpus distributional evidence does NOT confirm.

The architecture stays at: **CH and SH are two parallel humor-quality bases. Folios specialize in which one. The morphological elaboration level varies by folio (probably reflecting entry length/genre) but the underlying paradigm is identical.**

Without independent plant-ID or art-historical anchoring, we cannot bridge from CH:SH specialization to specific Avicennan humor identification.
