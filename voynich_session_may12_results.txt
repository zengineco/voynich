SESSION META-LESSON
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Vince was right to push back on the "FAILED" verdict for Test E. The
failure was in test design (wrong level of analysis), not in the
hypothesis. This is a recurring pattern in the project: claims labelled
LOCKED based on tests that turn out to have been malformed for the
later-understood structure of the data. The methodological lesson is
to label failures as "test failed AS STATED" rather than "hypothesis
failed", and revisit failed tests when our understanding of the data
structure changes (as H5 binyan finding changed it).

The corpus is now well-mapped at the morphological level. The next
move is into the semantic frontier — but ALWAYS via paradigm structure
and folio-level visual anchoring, never via raw guesswork about what
specific words "mean."
