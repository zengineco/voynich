THE BIG NUMBER
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

  Of 95 chor-bearing herbal-A paragraphs, 88 also contain chol.   92.6%
  Of 97 chol-bearing herbal-A paragraphs, 88 also contain chor.   90.7%

This is overwhelming. chor and chol are NOT mutually exclusive identity
markers. They CO-OCCUR in 90%+ of paragraphs that contain either.

If chor and chol were intrinsic temperaments (hot vs cold), we would
expect very few paragraphs with both — a plant is hot OR cold, not
both. We see the OPPOSITE: nearly every chor-bearing entry also has
chol, and vice versa.

VERDICT: The identity-model (each plant has a single intrinsic state)
is FALSIFIED for chor/chol. Whatever chor and chol are, they're NOT
mutually-exclusive intrinsic properties.

WHAT THIS LOOKS LIKE
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

The pattern in the data:
  - chor and chol are two POLES that co-occur 90%+ of the time
  - both can repeat freely (R-R, L-L adjacency very common)
  - they alternate freely (R-L, L-R common)
  - chody appears 35% of the time when both are present
  - chody almost NEVER appears adjacent to chor
  - chody often appears near chol

Reformulated reading: chor and chol describe TWO OPPOSED ACTIONS or
EFFECTS that the substance has. chody is a MODIFIER on the chol side
specifically.

This is not what you'd expect from Hot/Cold/Temperate. Galen's temperate
is symmetric — equidistant from hot and cold. chody is asymmetric — it
attaches to chol, not chor.

Your productive/stationary/null reading PARTIALLY fits:
  - chor as "productive/dynamic" — repeating, common, paired with chol
  - chol as "stationary/static" — repeating, common, attracts chody
  - chody as "null/null-of-chol" — modifier on the static pole only

This suggests TWO PRIMARY POLES with a QUALIFIER on one pole. Not
three symmetric states. The triad is actually a binary + a marker.

WHAT THIS PROBABLY ACTUALLY IS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Walking through what natural-medicine semantic systems look like that
would predict THIS pattern:

Candidate A: chor/chol = two opposed action-types (drawing/expelling,
  binding/loosening, warming/cooling action), with chody as a
  qualifier on the chol-pole (the cooling/binding/diminishing action,
  specifically).
  
Candidate B: chor/chol = two PHARMACOLOGICAL TARGETS (organ systems
  or body regions), with chody marking when one target gets a special
  treatment qualifier (e.g., "with diuretic action on the cold-target").

Candidate C: chor/chol = two preparation FORMS or USE-MODES (raw
  vs prepared, internal vs external, etc.), with chody marking a
  third sub-form.

Candidate D (Avicennan): chor/chol = the two QUALITY VECTORS
  (active = hot/cold, passive = dry/moist). chody marks the secondary
  passive vector. Each Canon entry says something like "It is hot in
  the second degree, and dry in the first" — TWO quality statements
  per entry. The chor/chol alternation could be the active/passive
  pair, with chody marking the passive intensification.

Candidate D is the cleanest fit with what Canon actually does.
EVERY Canon entry has TWO quality statements (a primary axis like
hot/cold, AND a secondary axis like dry/moist). They're independent
dimensions. They co-occur in every entry. They can repeat. Sometimes
there's a degree qualifier.

If chor encodes ACTIVE-quality (hot OR cold, marked by other morphology)
and chol encodes PASSIVE-quality (dry OR moist), and chody is a
secondary-passive modifier, the distributional pattern matches.
