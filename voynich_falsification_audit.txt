F6 — Is daiin really one word?  (controlling for asymmetry in left/right neighbors)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

If daiin is hiding a clause boundary, its left- and right-neighbor

  daiin (n=721)
    Top left-neighbors (prefix):  ch 19.7, qo 11.7, sh 7.4, ot 4.4, ok 3.9

daiin's neighbors are MORE SYMMETRIC than chedy's. chedy has a very strong
qo-prefix asymmetry (27% left, 38% right). daiin has roughly equal ch on

VERDICT: daiin is consistent with being a single token. Not a clause
boundary. The frequent right-da pattern (daiin.daiin... or daiin.daram...)

But: daiin's right-neighbor profile has higher diversity (more "other")

unit), so left-side context is constrained (only certain things precede a
unit-close) while right-side is more variable (anything can start the
next unit). Worth a deeper test.
